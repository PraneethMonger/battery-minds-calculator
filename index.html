Threshold Guide
🔴 N/P < 1.05 — Lithium plating risk. Unsafe. Increase anode loading. 🟡 1.05 – 1.10 — Marginal. Monitor closely. Aim higher. 🟢 1.10 – 1.30 — Optimal range. Recommended for all cell types. 🔵 > 1.30 — Over-balanced. Excess anode adds dead weight.

Spiral Winding Theory
Archimedean Spiral Electrode Length Formula
Single-layer stack thickness: t = 2×t_cat + t_Al + 2×t_ano + t_Cu + 2×t_sep [µm] Radial space: R = (D_cell/2) − (D_mandrel/2) [mm] Number of turns: N = floor(R / t_layer_mm) Electrode length (Archimedean): L = π × N × (D_mandrel + N × t_layer_mm) [mm] Derivation: L = Σ(2π × r_i) where r_i = r_core + i×t, i = 0..N−1 → L = 2π × [N×r_core + t×N(N−1)/2] = π×N×(2×r_core + (N−1)×t) Electrode width: W_cat = Winding_length − 2×Overhang; W_ano = Winding_length [mm] Electrode area: A = L × W [cm²] Areal capacity: C_areal = Active Loading × Specific Cap / 1000 [mAh/cm²] Cell capacity: C_cell = A × C_areal / 1000 [Ah] (double-sided coating)

⚡ Battery Minds Calculator — SP1.0.0

Designed and Developed by Kalangi Sai Praneeth with the assistance of AI

A comprehensive offline engineering tool for lithium-ion and advanced battery electrode characterization, cell balancing, and cell design. Supports LFP, NMC, LTO, LFP-LTO, Sodium-Ion, Silicon-Based, Solid-State, Supercapacitor and custom chemistries. Includes pouch and cylindrical (18650/21700) cell design modules with full spiral winding calculations.

⚙ Industry Standard Ranges Used for Validation

Cathode Loading: 10–25 mg/cm² (lab/coin), 15–35 mg/cm² (production)
Anode Loading (graphite): 5–18 mg/cm²
Cathode Porosity: 25–40% optimal; <20% over-calendered; >50% under-processed
Anode Porosity: 20–40% optimal (graphite)
N/P Ratio: 1.10–1.30 optimal; <1.05 unsafe; >1.30 over-balanced
Separator: 20–25 µm standard; 12–16 µm ceramic-coated
Pouch overhang: 1–2 mm per side (width & length)
Cylindrical mandrel: 2–4 mm; end-cap clearance 2–3 mm per side
